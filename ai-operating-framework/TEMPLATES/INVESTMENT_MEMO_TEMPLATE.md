> Use this to express a venture, product, or proposal in a concise investor-readable format.
# Investment Memo Template

## Memo title

[Insert title]

## Proposition

What is the venture, product, or opportunity in one clear sentence?

## The problem

What real problem or tension exists?

## The insight

What makes this idea worth attention now?

## The solution

What is being proposed?

## Why now

Why might this matter at this moment?
Examples:
- market timing
- behavioural shift
- regulatory change
- technical maturity
- cost shift

## Target user or customer

Who is the first credible adopter?

## Wedge

What is the sharpest entry point into the market?

## Business model direction

How might this create value or revenue?

## Market category

What category does this sit in today, and what new category might it create?

## Why this could win

What gives this proposition a credible chance of working?
Examples:
- founder insight
- distribution edge
- product differentiation
- timing advantage
- trust model

## Main risks

What could break the proposition?
Examples:
- regulation
- trust
- technical risk
- adoption friction
- economics

## Mitigation stance

How should those risks be reduced or approached?

## Stage of confidence

Choose one:
- concept only
- worth validating
- promising with evidence
- ready for deeper diligence

## Recommended next step

What should happen next?
Examples:
- customer validation
- legal review
- product design
- prototype
- investor conversations

## Bottom-line view

What is the honest overall assessment in two or three sentences?
