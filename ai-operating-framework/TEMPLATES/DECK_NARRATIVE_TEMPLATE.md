> Use this to structure the narrative arc of a proposal, pitch, or concept deck before visual design begins.
# Deck Narrative Template

## Deck title

[Insert title]

## Audience

Who is this deck for?
Examples:
- investor
- founder team
- internal sponsor
- partner
- design review

## Core message

What single message should the audience remember?

## Slide narrative

### Slide 1. Title and proposition

What is the title and one-line proposition?

### Slide 2. Problem

What problem, tension, or unmet need is being highlighted?

### Slide 3. Insight

What key insight makes this worth solving?

### Slide 4. Solution

What is being proposed?

### Slide 5. How it works

What simple flow or mechanism explains the concept?

### Slide 6. Why it matters

Why should the audience care?

### Slide 7. Target user or market

Who is the first credible adopter?

### Slide 8. Why this could win

What differentiates the proposition?

### Slide 9. Risks and realities

What should be acknowledged honestly?

### Slide 10. Roadmap or next steps

What happens next?

### Slide 11. The ask

What do you want from the audience?

## Optional slides

Use only if needed:
- business model
- market context
- founder advantage
- traction or evidence
- governance or compliance
- technical architecture

## Narrative checks

Before design begins, confirm:
- the story flows logically
- the language is honest
- the audience is clear
- the ask is explicit
- weak slides are removed rather than padded
